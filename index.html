Project Title: Personal Cybersecurity Awareness Guide

This project will create a clear and accessible cybersecurity guide that teaches everyday internet users how to protect themselves from common online threats such as phishing, weak passwords, and unsafe browsing. The guide will provide step-by-step tips, real-world examples, and best practices to promote safer digital habits. It will benefit students, professionals, and the general public by increasing awareness and reducing the risk of cyberattacks.

High-Level Scope
In / Out / Roadmap
▼

✅ In Scope for Semester Project

  • Research and explain safe browsing habits, including recognizing secure websites, avoiding malicious links, and understanding privacy settings.
  • Identify common signs of phishing emails and messages, and provide clear steps for verifying legitimate sources.
  • Emphasize the importance of creating strong, unique passwords and introduce methods for safely managing them (e.g., password managers).
  • Write, design, and format the Personal Cybersecurity Awareness Guide into a clear and visually engaging document.

🗺️ Roadmap Items (≤6 Months)

  • Add more advanced interactive learning features (e.g., progress tracking or achievements).
  • Expand the content library with new topics such as social media security and data privacy.
  • Translate the guide into other languages to reach a wider audience.
  • Publish and promote the website on professional platforms (e.g., LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio site).

❌ Out of Scope

  • Advanced cybersecurity training or hands-on coding tutorials.
  • Implementation of real cybersecurity tools or monitoring systems.
  • Large-scale marketing or turning the project into a national awareness campaign.

Deliverables Chart
Project outputs and definitions
▼
Deliverable SMART Description Definition of Done (DoD)
Research & Content Planning Complete research on 3 core cybersecurity topics (phishing, passwords, safe browsing) using at least 5 credible sources by Week 2, and create a detailed content outline with section headings and key points. Research documented with citations, content outline approved by instructor, key topics identified and organized into logical sections.
First Draft of Guide Write a complete first draft of the cybersecurity guide (minimum 8-10 pages) covering all planned topics with examples and explanations by Week 4. All sections written with clear explanations, at least 2 real-world examples per topic included, draft submitted to instructor for initial feedback.
Visual Design & Layout Design and format the guide using Canva or Google Docs by Week 5, incorporating at least 5 infographics, consistent branding, and accessible color scheme (WCAG AA compliant). Guide is visually appealing, all graphics created/sourced, formatting is consistent throughout, accessibility checklist completed.
Peer Review & Usability Testing Conduct usability testing with 3-5 peers and family members by Week 6, collecting feedback on clarity, readability, and usefulness using a structured feedback form. Feedback collected from minimum 3 testers, feedback form completed, list of improvement areas documented with priority rankings.
Technical Review & Validation Have content reviewed by IT Club member or cybersecurity expert by Week 6 to ensure technical accuracy of all security recommendations and best practices. Technical reviewer completes review checklist, all inaccuracies corrected, expert sign-off or approval received.
Final Polished Guide (PDF/Digital Format) Complete the final version of the cybersecurity awareness guide incorporating all feedback by Week 7, exported as a professional PDF with working hyperlinks and table of contents. All feedback implemented, guide is error-free (spelling/grammar check completed), PDF is professionally formatted and accessible, file size optimized for sharing.
Project Reflection & Presentation Create a 5-minute presentation summarizing the project process, lessons learned, and key takeaways by Week 7, along with a 2-page written reflection. Presentation slides completed, reflection document addresses challenges and learnings, presented to class or submitted to instructor.
